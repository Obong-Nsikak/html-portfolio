Dora Akunyili

Dora Akunyili, a Nigerian pharmacist, and former Director-General of the National Agency for Food and Drug Administration and Control (NAFDAC).

Dora Akunyili (1954–2014) was a Nigerian pharmacist and government official. She served as the Director-General of the National Agency for Food and Drug Administration and Control (NAFDAC) in Nigeria from 2001 to 2008. During her tenure, she gained international recognition for her efforts to combat counterfeit drugs and improve the quality of pharmaceutical products in Nigeria.

Here is time line of Dr. Dora Akunyili

  • Dora Akunyili was born in 14 July 1954, Makurdi
  • 1978. She received her certificate in pharmacology at the University of Nigeria (U.N.N.)
  • 1985. Received her Ph.D. in ethnopharmacology
  • 2001. She was appointed NAFDAC DG
  • Time magazine award 2006 ("One of the eighteen heroes of our time")
  • Person of the Year 2005 Award – Silverbird Communications Ltd, Lagos, 5 January 2006
  • Award of Excellence – Integrated World Services (IWS), December 2005
  • Award of Excellence – Advocacy for Democracy Dividends International, Lagos, 17 December 2005
  • Meritorious Award 2005 – St. Michael's Military Catholic Church, Apapa, Lagos, 4 December 2005
  • African Virtuous and Entrepreneurial Women Merit Award 2005 – African Biographical Network, December, 2005
  • Award for the Best Government Parastatal – National Association of Nigerian Students (NANS), December, 2005
  • An Icon of Excellence Award – The African Cultural Institute and Zenith Bank PLC,[42] 8 December 2005
  • 2005 Grassroots Human Rights Campaigner Award London-based Human Rights Defense Organization, 8 December. 2005
  • Most Innovative Director Award Federal Government College, Ijanikin, Lagos, October, 2005
  • Integrity Award 2003 – Transparency International.
  • 2012, her book: The War Against Counterfeit Medicine: My Story was published
  • 7 June 2014. Dora Akunyili died at a specialist cancer hospital in India after a two-year battle with uterine cancer
